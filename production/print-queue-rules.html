Then how many pages?

Every group keys above defaults to 1 page regardless of qty. Manager splits manually.

Default rule
1 page per group, no matter the quantity.
Qty 10 of one fixture in one color → still 1 page. Lead clicks +1 (Split) on the queue page if work needs to be parallelized.
Lead override
Edit the page number directly on the queue page.
Edits persist in your browser via localStorage — they survive the hourly auto-refresh. A "reset" link in the page subheader clears them.

Worked examples

Same order, walked through the rules above.

Example A · Color, two finishes
31000-1K (Brass + Barely) + 31000-2K (Black + Barely)
Both lines are color products → Powder Coating. Both share color = Barely → group key ("color", "Barely").
1 printout (parts paint together). 2 Notion rows (Brass and Black builds are different).
Example B · Sample-only order
SAC-PG (Python Green sample)
Whole order is samples → short-circuit.
1 printout · Shipping. 0 Notion rows.
Example C · Mixed sample + fixture
SAC-PG + 31000-1K
Sample is held — not its own page, not a Notion row. Fixture line follows the color-product branch.
1 printout · Powder Coating. 1 Notion row.
Example D · Two SKUs, same color
30220-2F (47" Slate Blue) + 31000-1F (32" Slate Blue)
Both color products in Slate Blue → one paint group across SKUs.
1 printout (key ("color", "Slate Blue") covers both). 2 Notion rows (different parent SKUs).
Example E · Accessories only
ER1-3-1 (rod) + BU (bushing)
No lighting or hardware in the order. Both lines = accessory.
2 printouts · Shipping (one per SKU). 2 Notion rows.
Example F · Mixed accessory + fixture
ER1-3-1 (rod) + 31000-1 (chandelier)
Rod row routes to Assembly + flagged for Catherine to confirm Shipping vs Assembly. Chandelier row to Assembly normally.
2 printouts · Assembly. 2 Notion rows.
