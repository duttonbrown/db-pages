Marketing Truth Audit · Q1 2026

What we can actually trust from each ad platform

Every ad platform claims credit for sales it influenced — and they all double-claim. Trust coefficients tell us how much of each platform's reported revenue is real.

← Back to Marketing

The idea in one line

A trust coefficient is the fraction of a platform's claimed revenue that an independent referee (GA4) actually agrees was theirs.

Trust Coefficient = GA4-attributed revenue ÷ Platform-reported revenue

Below 1.0 means the platform is over-claiming. Below 0.1 means most of what they report is fiction.

Why each platform inflates differently

Meta 0.034

iOS 14.5 (April 2021) gutted Meta's tracking. Most "conversions" today are statistical models — algorithmic guesses, not observed events.

Tell: we scaled Meta spend 60% Jan→Mar, but GA4-attributed Meta revenue stayed flat at ~$3K/mo.

Pinterest 0.23

Pinterest's 7-day-click / 1-day-view window claims credit for casual exposure. Someone scrolls past a pin, then buys via Google search five days later — Pinterest claims it.

Tell: "17× retargeting ROAS" is largely customers who'd have bought anyway.

Google ~0.85

Mostly click-based at moments of high purchase intent. People searching "modern brass chandelier" are genuinely shopping. GA4 mostly agrees.

Caveat: brand search ("dutton brown") may overlap with organic — worth a pause test.

Email ~1.0

First-party action — someone clicked a link in an email we sent. No third-party tracking gymnastics, no double-claiming.

Caveat: Omnisend self-reported revenue still pending — coefficient is estimated, not yet verified.

Methodology — and where to be skeptical

Window mismatch — to fix on next run

Meta and Google spend numbers were Q1 strict (Jan–Mar). Pinterest used a rolling 90-day window (Jan 30–Apr 30). GA4 revenue was pulled on the 90-day window for all channels. Same-window comparisons would shift Meta's coefficient slightly (toward 0.021 — even worse) and would make Google look marginally better.

Bottom line: the directional finding (Meta is broken, Google is honest, Pinterest is in between) is solid. The exact numbers will move when we re-run with strict matching windows + multiple attribution models.

Data sources used

Meta spend from AdScale CSV (monthly rollup). Google spend via Pipeboard / Google Ads API. Pinterest via Pinterest Marketing API v5. GA4 via Data API, property 342056181, last-non-direct cross-channel. Shopify ground-truth not yet pulled — every coefficient has an uncertainty band.

What to do next

  1. Quadruple down on email. $47K Q1 revenue at near-zero cost. Every other budget decision should ask: does this build the email list?
  2. Apply for Meta + Pinterest Conversion Lift Studies. Free, ~3 weeks. The only way to prove incrementality vs. attribution. Don't scale either platform until results are in.
  3. Run a 30-day Meta pause test. If GA4 Meta revenue stays flat when we cut Meta spend in half, the inflation thesis is confirmed.
  4. Pivot Pinterest paid toward trade leads + sample SKUs. Quality-over-quantity bets that fit Pinterest's nature as a discovery platform.
  5. Re-run the audit with matched windows + multiple GA4 attribution models (data-driven, last-click, first-click) to see how channel ranking shifts under different assumptions.
  6. Pull Shopify ground-truth Q1 revenue so we know how much GA4 itself is missing — then every coefficient gets an honest uncertainty range.
